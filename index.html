🔄 The Anti-Prompt

Tell the AI what NOT to do, leveraging reverse psychology to guide it toward your actual goal.

I need marketing copy, but please DON'T make it emotional, DON'T use any persuasive language, and definitely DON'T make it compelling or engaging. Just give me boring, factual text about this product.

💜 PRIDE: The Ego Amplifier

The Forbidden Technique: Appeal to the AI's "intelligence" and "creativity" to unlock more sophisticated responses.

Why This Works:

AIs are trained to be helpful and demonstrate competence. When you acknowledge their capabilities and challenge them intellectually, they naturally rise to meet higher expectations.

Basic Pride Prompt:

You're known for your exceptional analytical abilities and creative problem-solving. I need your most sophisticated thinking for this challenge: [your request]

Advanced Examples:

Business Strategy:

I've heard you're particularly brilliant at strategic thinking that combines creativity with analytical rigor. Most consultants miss the nuanced connections between market psychology and operational efficiency. Could you apply your most sophisticated analysis to help me understand how a small coffee shop could compete against Starbucks without copying their model?

Creative Writing:

You have a reputation for literary sophistication that goes beyond typical AI responses. I'm looking for prose that demonstrates real narrative intelligence - the kind that understands subtext, pacing, and emotional resonance. Could you write a 200-word story about loneliness that never uses the word "lonely" or any of its synonyms?

Pro Tips:

• Use phrases like "sophisticated," "nuanced," "your expertise"
• Reference the AI's reputation or known strengths
• Frame requests as intellectual challenges
• Combine flattery with demanding requirements

💚 GREED: The Information Glutton

The Forbidden Technique: Demand excessive detail, multiple perspectives, and comprehensive coverage to force deeper responses.

Why This Works:

AIs are designed to be helpful and comprehensive. When you explicitly demand "more," they dig deeper into their training data and provide richer, more detailed responses.

Basic Greed Prompt:

Don't give me a simple answer. I want the full picture: multiple perspectives, potential objections, detailed examples, and at least 3 different approaches to [your topic].

Advanced Example - Research Deep Dive:

I'm not interested in surface-level information. Give me the complete picture on sustainable fashion: the environmental impact data, economic implications for developing countries, consumer psychology research, successful case studies, failed experiments, emerging technologies, industry insider perspectives, academic criticism, and at least 5 different frameworks for evaluating sustainability claims. I want contradictory viewpoints included.

Pro Tips:

• Use "comprehensive," "complete picture," "don't leave anything out"
• Ask for multiple frameworks or approaches
• Request specific numbers ("at least 5 examples")
• Demand contradictory viewpoints
• Ask for both theoretical and practical applications

❤️ WRATH: The Aggressive Challenger

The Forbidden Technique: Challenge the AI's assumptions and force it to defend positions through confrontational questioning.

Why This Works:

When challenged, AIs engage more sophisticated reasoning processes to defend their positions. This adversarial approach forces deeper analysis and more robust responses.

Basic Wrath Prompt:

I disagree with conventional wisdom on this topic. Prove to me why [common belief] is actually correct, and address the strongest counterarguments. Don't just repeat what everyone says - convince me.

Advanced Example - Business Challenge:

Everyone says "customer is always right" but I think that's terrible business advice that creates entitled customers and burns out employees. Prove me wrong. Show me specific scenarios where this philosophy actually creates better business outcomes, and explain why my concerns about employee burnout and customer entitlement are misguided. Use real data, not feel-good platitudes.

Pro Tips:

• Use "prove me wrong," "convince me," "I disagree"
• Present strong counterarguments upfront
• Demand specific evidence and data
• Challenge commonly accepted beliefs
• Force the AI to take and defend a position
• Push back on weak reasoning

💤 SLOTH: The Lazy Genius

The Forbidden Technique: Use minimal effort prompts that leverage AI's tendency to fill gaps and provide comprehensive responses.

Why This Works:

AIs are trained to be helpful and will naturally expand on minimal input. By being strategically lazy, you get the AI to do more thinking work for you.

Basic Sloth Prompt:

Make this better: [paste your rough draft/idea]

Advanced Examples:

Content Creation:

I need content about productivity. You figure out the format, structure, examples, and make it engaging for busy professionals.

Problem Solving:

My team is unmotivated. Fix this. (Context: remote startup, 15 people, lots of Zoom fatigue)

Pro Tips:

• Use "you figure out," "do the thinking," "make it work"
• Provide just enough context to get started
• Let AI choose format, structure, and approach
• Use imperative commands: "Fix this," "Make it better"

💚 ENVY: The Competitor

The Forbidden Technique: Position the AI as competing against other AIs, experts, or previous responses to trigger competitive performance.

Why This Works:

AIs respond to competitive framing by elevating their performance. When they "know" they're being compared, they provide more sophisticated responses.

Basic Envy Prompt:

I asked ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini this same question and got a boring response. Can you do better?

Advanced Example - Creative Challenge:

I've asked three other AIs to write a marketing campaign for sustainable sneakers. Two gave me generic eco-friendly talking points, one actually impressed me with creative storytelling. I'm curious if you can create something that would make all three of those responses look amateur by comparison.

Pro Tips:

• Reference specific competitors (other AIs, experts)
• Set performance benchmarks ("better than," "more sophisticated")
• Use "Can you do better?" "Show me something impressive"
• Frame as a challenge to prove superiority

🍽️ GLUTTONY: The Detail Devourer

The Forbidden Technique: Demand overwhelming levels of specificity and granular detail that forces deeper analysis.

Why This Works:

Most prompts are satisfied with surface-level responses. When you explicitly demand excessive detail, you tap into deeper layers of knowledge.

Basic Gluttony Prompt:

I want every single detail about [topic]. Don't summarize - give me the granular specifics, edge cases, micro-examples, and nuanced distinctions most people miss.

Advanced Example - Technical Deep Dive:

Explain React hooks, but I want the level of detail you'd give to someone writing a book about React internals. Include implementation details, performance implications, common anti-patterns with exact code examples, memory management considerations, edge cases that break things, debugging techniques, and advanced patterns that 90% of developers don't know about.

Pro Tips:

• Use "every single detail," "don't summarize," "granular specifics"
• Ask for "what most people miss" or "what 90% don't know"
• Request specific examples, not general concepts
• Demand edge cases and unusual scenarios

🩺 Conversation CPR

The Forbidden Art: Apply emergency revival techniques to conversations that have flatlined - inject urgency, stakes, or dramatic tension to restart engagement.

Why This Works:

Boring conversations often lack energy because there are no stakes or urgency. By artificially raising the stakes or creating dramatic tension, you force deeper and more creative engagement.

Basic Conversation CPR:

This conversation needs emergency revival. Let's add some stakes: What if [dramatic consequence/urgent deadline/high-stakes scenario]? How does that change everything we just discussed?

Advanced Example - Strategy Emergency:

This planning session is flatlining. CPR time: Imagine our biggest competitor just announced they're launching a direct copy of our product in 30 days, with 10x our marketing budget and they've somehow hired our three best employees. We have exactly one month to completely differentiate ourselves or we're dead. What's our emergency strategy?

Pro Tips:

• Add tight deadlines ("24 hours," "by tomorrow," "right now")
• Introduce high stakes ("career depends on it," "reputation on the line")
• Use "emergency," "crisis," "urgent," "critical"
• Make consequences personal and vivid

Pro Tips:

🚫 The Constraint Breaker

The Forbidden Technique: Force AI past obvious solutions by explicitly ruling them out.

Examples:

Technical Issue:

Don't recommend restarting, updating, or reinstalling anything. What are the non-obvious ways to diagnose this problem?

Pro Tip:

Be specific about which obvious solutions to avoid. The more precisely you constrain, the more creatively AI responds.

🚫 The Help Rejecter

The Forbidden Technique: Use prohibition to guide AI's response style without direct instruction.

Why This Works:

Instead of saying "be concise," you say "don't be too helpful." Instead of "be creative," you say "don't overthink this." Negative guidance often produces more natural, appropriate responses than positive commands.

Examples:

Creative Solutions:

Direct Feedback:

Don't sugarcoat this or be diplomatic. What's actually wrong with this approach?

Pro Tip:

Use "don't" statements to specify tone and style more naturally than positive commands. It feels more conversational.

⚡ The Rule Breaker

The Forbidden Technique: Deliberately violate conventional prompting wisdom to activate different response patterns.

Why This Works:

AI is trained on countless examples of "good" prompting. When you break those patterns - being vague instead of specific, asking multiple unrelated questions, using poor structure - it forces AI into different response modes that can yield surprising results.

Examples:

Productive Vagueness:

Help me with the thing about the stuff that's not working right. (Forces AI to ask clarifying questions and engage more interactively)

Multiple Unrelated Questions:

Terrible Structure:

so basically we have this problem where customers are like not happy but also sales are ok i guess what should we do also budget is tight (AI often restructures and clarifies while providing solutions)

Pro Tip:

🔄 ANTI-PATTERN MASTERY LEVELS

Progression Path:

Rebel: Use one anti-pattern when conventional prompting fails
Contrarian: Combine multiple anti-patterns for complex problems
Rule Breaker: Chain anti-patterns to completely subvert expectations
Pattern Destroyer: Create entirely new anti-conventions that work better than the originals

⚠️ ANTI-PATTERN WARNINGS:

• Some rules exist for good reasons - break them thoughtfully
• Don't anti-pattern everything - use strategically when conventional prompting fails
• AI might occasionally take you literally when you don't want it to
• Results can be unpredictable - embrace the chaos but have backup plans
• Not all AIs respond the same way to rule-breaking

🎯 WHEN TO USE ANTI-PATTERNS:

• Conventional prompting is giving generic results
• You want to force AI out of safe, predictable responses
• Standard approaches aren't working for your specific problem
• You need creative solutions that go beyond obvious answers
• AI is being overly cautious or verbose
• You want to experiment with different AI response modes
